# Orbit: The Brand Ecosystem OS

## 1. Product Concept
**Orbit** moves beyond linear project management (Asana) or generic whiteboards (Miro) by creating a "Spatial Operating System" specifically for marketers. 

The core philosophy is **Contextual Strategy**. In traditional tools, the "Strategy" (a doc) is separated from the "Execution" (a task). In Orbit, the Strategy Card sits physically next to the Post Card, linked by visual proximity and data.

## 2. Additional Smart Card Types
These cards are implemented (or prepared for) in the codebase:

1.  **The Oracle (AI Strategy Card)**
    *   **Problem:** Writer's block and lack of strategic alignment.
    *   **Feature:** Input a topic/goal, utilizes Google Gemini to generate 5-10 distinct angles/hooks.
    *   **Why:** Turns the board into an active participant, not just a passive record.

2.  **Live Pulse (Analytics Card)**
    *   **Problem:** disconnecting creative work from performance data.
    *   **Feature:** specific KPI visualizations (ROAS, CTR, Impressions) using D3/Recharts.
    *   **Why:** Forces creative teams to design with data in view.

3.  **Persona Profile**
    *   **Problem:** Losing sight of who the customer is during asset production.
    *   **Feature:** A rigid, always-visible card with Avatar, Pains, and Gains.
    *   **Why:** anchored visual reminder of the target audience.

4.  **Asset DAM (Digital Asset Manager)**
    *   **Problem:** Google Drive folders are hard to browse visually.
    *   **Feature:** A mini-grid of approved brand assets that can be dragged into Post Cards.

5.  **Campaign Timeline**
    *   **Problem:** Gantt charts are too heavy for social.
    *   **Feature:** A simplified horizontal timeline card to visualize flight dates.

## 3. Strategic & Aesthetic Values
*   **Strategic Thinking:** By placing "Why" cards (Strategy/Persona) next to "What" cards (Posts), users implicitly check their work against the goal.
*   **Visual Premium:** Glassmorphism, subtle shadows, and distinct "blocks" (not sticky notes) make it feel like a control room, not a messy desk.
*   **Scale:** Tabs allow breaking down by Quarter or Channel, preventing the "infinite mess" syndrome.
